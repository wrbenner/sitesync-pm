## The Five Universal Pains (Proven Across Every Platform)

These are not opinions. They appear in thousands of reviews across every construction software product. Fix all five and you win.

### Pain 1: Complexity That Kills Adoption
**Evidence:** Procore requires 3-6 months to become proficient. Even then, teams use 20-30% of features. Buildertrend: "32 screens from initial lead to final invoice." G2 repeatedly: "feels overwhelming."

**The opportunity:** A product so simple a superintendent with dirty hands can use it on day 1 — with AI that explains what to do next in plain English. SiteSync's AI Copilot eliminates the learning curve entirely. This alone is worth switching for.

**Build priority:** Adaptive onboarding (see below). AI guide mode on every screen.

---

### Pain 2: Broken Mobile
**Evidence:** 57% of Buildertrend negative reviews mention app crashes, slow loads, limited features. Procore: "frustrated by the offline experience." G2: "slow or completely fails to function." Users resort to "using the mobile web browser instead of the app."

**The opportunity:** A mobile app that works offline, loads instantly, and has 44px touch targets that work with work gloves. This is SiteSync's single biggest differentiator for field teams.

**Build priority:** P0. Nothing matters if the mobile app doesn't work on a jobsite without WiFi.

---

### Pain 3: Financial Workflows That Don't Work
**Evidence:** Across every platform: "preparing a pay application is time-consuming," "had to create a spreadsheet for budget," "financial module too far behind QuickBooks/Xero," "QuickBooks integration causes issues," "sales tax randomly spread across cost codes." One user: "$15,000 in Procore fees and the Xero integration never worked."

**The opportunity:** AIA G702/G703 that generates in one click. QuickBooks sync that actually works. Job costing in real time. SiteSync already has G702/G703 PDF generation and Stripe Connect. This is the feature that gets CFOs to approve the switch.

**Build priority:** P1. The financial engine unlocks the enterprise buyer.

---

### Pain 4: Subs Are Forced Users, Not Real Users
**Evidence:** Reddit thread: "GCs mandate you to use their Procore. You're juggling 10 different logins, doing double data entry. The software primarily serves GC compliance needs, not subcontractor profitability." Universal: "What about MY data for future bids? What about when I'M owed? What about MY schedule across multiple GCs?"

**The opportunity:** Give subs their OWN view. Not a compliance portal — a profitability portal. Show them: what work is scheduled this week, what's owed to them, retainage held across all projects, their payment history for future financing. Make this free. Subs will ask GCs to use SiteSync. This reverses the mandate dynamic.

**Build priority:** P1. This creates the network effect that makes SiteSync grow without a sales team.

---

### Pain 5: Data Hostage
**Evidence:** Capterra: "no simple or bulk way to download years' worth of files." Reddit: "you risk losing all project history." Buildertrend specifically designed to make export painful.

**The opportunity:** Make data export so easy it's embarrassing. One-click export to CSV, PDF, ZIP. Full project archive in under 60 seconds. Paradoxically, making it easy to leave creates trust that makes people want to stay. This is SiteSync's moral high ground over every competitor.

**Build priority:** P2 (but mention prominently in marketing).

## What "Dynamic and For Anyone" Actually Means

The data shows a clear pattern: the highest-rated construction software products are SPECIALIZED (JobTread 4.7, Buildxact 4.8, AccuLynx 4.4). Not because being generic is bad — but because generic products overwhelm users with irrelevant features.

**The solution is ADAPTIVE UX, not feature reduction.**

SiteSync has all the features. The question is: which features does each user see first?

### The Persona Configuration Matrix

| User Type | What They See | What's Hidden |
|-----------|--------------|---------------|
| **General Contractor** | Full platform: RFIs, Submittals, BIM, Budget, AI Agents | Nothing hidden |
| **Subcontractor** | My Tasks, My Schedule, RFI Responses, Payment Status, Daily Log | BIM, Estimating, Portfolio |
| **Specialty Trade** (electrician, plumber) | Schedule, Time Tracking, Invoice, Customer Communication | BIM, Submittals, Complex PM |
| **Owner/Developer** | Budget vs. Actual, Schedule, Progress Photos, Draw Reports | Field tools, PM workflows |
| **Residential Builder** | Client Portal, Selections, Change Orders, Schedule, Photos | BIM, AIA billing, Certified Payroll |
| **Project Engineer** | RFIs, Submittals, Drawings, RFI Ball-in-Court | Financial, Portfolio |

**Implementation:** Onboarding wizard asks three questions:
1. "What best describes your company?" (GC / Sub / Specialty Trade / Owner / Residential)
2. "How many projects are you managing?" (1-5 / 6-20 / 20+)
3. "What's your biggest pain today?" (Financial chaos / Field communication / Document management / I'm just starting)

The sidebar, dashboard widgets, and navigation reorganize based on answers. Every user thinks SiteSync was built specifically for them.

## The White Space Nobody Owns

These are the features the market is asking for that NO product currently does well:

1. **Sub profitability portal** — not compliance, THEIR data, THEIR payments, THEIR schedule across all GCs. Nobody has built this for subs.

2. **AI that explains construction** — not just AI that automates. A superintendent who doesn't know what an RFI is should be able to ask "what do I do here?" and get a plain English answer. The AI copilot knows construction.

3. **Pricing that doesn't punish growth** — Procore's ACV model penalizes revenue growth. SiteSync's model (flat base + transaction fee) should reward volume.

4. **Zero-setup demo** — "they don't give you time to learn before you have to pay." SiteSync should load with a pre-built sample project (Riverside Tower) so every prospect sees a fully-featured product in 30 seconds.

5. **The GC-mandated sub gets value** — currently, subs using Procore because their GC mandates it get NOTHING. If the mandate brings them to SiteSync and they immediately see their payment status, their schedule, their retainage — the mandate is no longer a burden. It's a benefit.
