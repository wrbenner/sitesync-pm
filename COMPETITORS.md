# Competitive Intelligence

The engine reads this before every audit. Use it to understand what SiteSync must beat.

## Procore (The Incumbent King)

Market: Enterprise GCs, $50M+ annual construction volume. ~$25K+/year.

What they do well:
- RFI workflow with email reply (architects respond without logging in)
- Quick Capture: video to punch item with automatic transcription
- Change orders created directly from RFIs (tight workflow integration)
- Unlimited users on every plan (no per-seat friction)
- 300+ integrations marketplace
- Procore Assist (conversational AI that searches specs, RFIs, submittals, building codes)
- Procore Agent Builder: no-code AI agents for drafting RFIs, managing submittals, generating daily log summaries

Where Procore fails (SiteSync opportunities):
- Pricing is opaque and expensive ($25K+/year based on annual construction volume)
- Modules don't integrate well with each other despite being "unified"
- Steep learning curve, requires multi-day training
- Mobile app is an adapted desktop app, not truly field-first
- Financial reporting requires workarounds and manual exports
- Customer support is slow and generic
- Offline mode is limited
- No predictive scheduling (no AI that says "this will be late by Thursday")
- Cash flow forecasting is not native
- Lien waiver tracking is bolted on, not part of the core financial flow

## Autodesk Construction Cloud (The Design Giant)

Market: Design-build firms, BIM-heavy projects, large commercial.

What they do well:
- Best BIM coordination in the industry (Revit/AutoCAD integration)
- Automatic clash detection with Navisworks integration
- Model-based issue tracking (issues linked to 3D geometry, not just plans)
- Construction IQ: AI risk prediction from project data
- Unified design-to-build workflow

Where ACC fails (SiteSync opportunities):
- Field experience is second class (PlanGrid acquisition never fully integrated)
- Financial tools are underpowered compared to Procore
- Mobile experience is clunky on job sites
- Learning curve is steep for non-BIM users
- No native daily log or field capture workflow
- No construction-specific financial intelligence (AIA billing, lien waivers, retainage)
- Offline support is limited

## Fieldwire (The Field-First Challenger)

Market: Subcontractors, foremen, small-mid GCs. Per-user pricing.

What they do well:
- True mobile-first design (opens to project page, swipe gestures, Apple Pencil markup)
- Excellent offline functionality (full feature set offline, auto-sync)
- Two-step verification on punch items (quality control built in)
- Task-to-plan linking (pin tasks to drawing locations)
- QR code integration for physical-to-digital linking
- Fast adoption among field crews

Where Fieldwire fails (SiteSync opportunities):
- No financial tools at all (no budget, no billing, no cost tracking)
- No BIM or 3D capabilities
- No enterprise portfolio view
- Limited integrations (QuickBooks only)
- No AI features
- Not designed for PM-level workflows (RFIs, submittals, change orders are basic)
- No schedule/Gantt capabilities

## Buildertrend (The Residential King)

Market: Residential builders and remodelers, 2-50 employees. $299/month.

What they do well:
- All-in-one for residential (CRM, scheduling, financial, client portal)
- Client portal with transparent cost tracking
- Dedicated account managers and onboarding coaches
- 60% cheaper than Procore
- Good Gantt scheduling with budget integration

Where Buildertrend fails:
- No commercial construction workflows
- No real-time field-to-office RFI/submittal workflows
- Basic drawing version management
- No portfolio management
- Weak mobile field experience
- No AI features

## Emerging AI Threats

ALICE Technologies: AI schedule optimization, what-if scenario testing. Enterprise.
Buildots: Helmet cameras + computer vision for progress tracking. Claims 50% delay reduction.
OpenSpace: Visual intelligence, AI-powered field image analysis.
PermitFlow: AI permitting automation. $54M Series B from Accel.
Opusense AI: Voice-to-text field reports, auto-organized with photos.

## The Gap SiteSync Fills

Nobody combines all of these in one platform:
1. Field-first mobile UX (Fieldwire level)
2. Enterprise financial depth (Procore level)
3. AI-native intelligence (beyond anything shipping today)
4. Beautiful, intuitive design (Linear/Stripe level)
5. Predictive capabilities (schedule, cost, risk)
6. Construction domain depth (lien waivers, AIA billing, CSI job costing)

Procore is complex and expensive. ACC is design-centric and field-weak. Fieldwire has no financials. Buildertrend is residential only. Nobody has real AI woven into every workflow.

That is the opportunity. Build for the superintendent who is tired of Procore's complexity, the PM who needs real financial intelligence, and the CFO who wants numbers they can trust.
