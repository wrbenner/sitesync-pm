---
title: Two-factor authentication (2FA)
description: How to enable, manage, and recover TOTP-based MFA on your SiteSync account.
---

Two-factor authentication adds a second proof of identity beyond your password. Even if your password leaks, an attacker without your authenticator app can't sign in.

## Who should enable it

Every user benefits, but it's strongly recommended (and may be required by your organization's policy) if you hold any of these roles:

- **Owner** — full org-wide access
- **Admin** — manage users, billing, and project settings
- **Project Manager** — modify scope, change orders, pay applications

If you hold one of these roles and haven't enrolled, you'll see a banner across the top of every page until you do.

## What you'll need

A TOTP-compatible authenticator app on your phone or laptop. Any of these work:

- **1Password** (recommended — handles backup automatically)
- **Apple Passwords** (built into iOS 17+ and macOS Sonoma+)
- **Google Authenticator**
- **Authy** (Twilio)
- **Microsoft Authenticator**

## How to enable

1. Sign in to SiteSync PM and click your avatar in the top-right.
2. Choose **Profile**.
3. Scroll to **Two-factor authentication** and click **Enable**.
4. Scan the QR code with your authenticator app, or paste the secret manually.
5. Type the 6-digit code your app generates and click **Verify and enable**.

You'll see a green "Enabled" badge once the code verifies. From now on, signing in will prompt you for the 6-digit code after your password.

## Removing or replacing your authenticator

1. Open **Profile**.
2. Under **Two-factor authentication**, click **Remove**.
3. Confirm.
4. Re-enroll with the new device using the steps above.

<Warning>
Removing your authenticator weakens your account security. Have your replacement ready before you remove the existing one.
</Warning>

## Recovery if you lose your phone

Email `support@sitesync.app` from the email on your account. We will:

1. Verify your identity via a video call.
2. Reset the MFA factor on your account.
3. Walk you through re-enrollment immediately.

There is no self-serve recovery code — the friction is intentional. An attacker who phishes one of your colleagues should not be able to bypass MFA via a "lost phone" form.

## Behind the scenes

We use Supabase Auth's TOTP enrollment, which generates a per-user 32-character base-32 secret and produces standard `otpauth://` QR codes (RFC 6238). Codes are validated server-side; we never trust client-supplied "verified" claims.
