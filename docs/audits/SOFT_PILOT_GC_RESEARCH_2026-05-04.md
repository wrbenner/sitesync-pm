## 1. Carleton Companies

- **HQ / regions.** Founded 1991 in Dallas, TX by Printice L. Gary (formerly of Trammell Crow Residential). Primary operating base Dallas, with a listed Boston office (56 Broad St, Suite 14070). Project footprint is concentrated across the South and Southwest US, with a heavy Texas weighting (Dallas, Austin, Lockhart, Corinth, Celina). Property-management arm operates across roughly 20 states.
- **Scale.** Privately held, no ENR Top 400 listing surfaced. Self-reported portfolio: 24,000–35,000 multifamily units developed/built across 30+ years (numbers vary across the corporate site and Carleton Construction subsidiary copy). Bonding capacity stated at $120M, multi-state. No public revenue or headcount; LinkedIn listing for Carleton Construction is small (low-hundreds employees, mostly site/PM staff).
- **PM tools (signals).** No public mention of Procore, Autodesk Build, or Fieldwire on their site, blog, or press. Bluebook ProView and Levelset listings are present but don't disclose tooling. Affordable-housing GCs of this size typically run a hybrid stack — Procore or Sage 300 CRE for accounting, Excel/email for daily logs and RFIs. **Treat as unknown but likely under-tooled.**
- **Project types.** Affordable + mixed-income multifamily, new construction and rehab. LIHTC-funded deals (their first transaction was a 192-unit Cedar Ridge buy funded by LIHTC equity). Public-private partnerships with housing authorities — Housing Authority of the City of Austin (HACA) is a recurring client (Pathways at Chalmers South, ongoing Chalmers Courts transformation). LEED Platinum on at least one project. **Not commercial, not industrial — pure multifamily affordable.**
- **Leadership / names.**
  - Printice L. Gary — founder, Managing Partner. Also serves on the board of Preservation of Affordable Housing, Inc. Trade-press visible.
  - Neal Hildebrandt and Jeff Fulenchek — co-managing partners.
  - Tasha Krawiec — leads Property Management division.
- **Public pain points.** Glassdoor has ~9–10 reviews; one Sr. Project Accountant flagged "extremely rude" onsite team and "non-existent training practices." Small sample, but the training gap is the kind of seam an AI co-pilot like Iris is built for. No lawsuits, project-delay news, or trade-press complaints surfaced.
- **Recent project announcements (last 12 mo).** Gateway Oak Cliff (Dallas) — 230-unit mixed-income, ribbon-cut July 2024. Chalmers Courts redevelopment (Austin, with HACA) is ongoing. Pipeline mentions of Celina (2026) and Corinth, Lockhart, Austin (2025). Most recent groundbreaking press release on PRWeb is from 2018, so they don't push press hard.
- **Digital adoption signals.** No "VDC" or "BIM" team listed. No tech blog, no construction-tech case studies, no LinkedIn posts evangelizing software. They do market LEED/green-building expertise. **Read: traditional, relationship-driven shop, light digital footprint.**

## Recommendation for the pilot playbook

Both targets are small, light-tech, Texas-anchored shops — exactly the SiteSync ICP. Differences that matter:

| Dimension | Carleton | Nexus (Houston) |
|---|---|---|
| Size | Larger (multi-state, $120M bonding) | Smaller (Houston + OC, ~dozens of staff) |
| Project type | Multifamily affordable / LIHTC | Commercial interiors + public works / university |
| Money workflows | Pay apps + LIHTC draws (cents-precision matters a lot) | Lump-sum + GMP commercial |
| Iris fit | RFI follow-ups, daily logs, pay-app drafts all hit hard on LIHTC compliance paperwork | Daily logs and RFI follow-ups for university owners (procurement-heavy) |
| Outreach surface area | Printice Gary publicly visible, board of POAH — warm-intro target | No clear executive contact; cold via Cody Rutledge or website form |
| Risk | Affordable-housing audit trail = high stakes if Iris drafts something wrong | Smaller blast radius, lower-stakes pilot |

**My read:** Nexus (Houston) is the safer 14-day pilot — smaller blast radius, less compliance-heavy workflows, easier to recover if Iris drafts a bad RFI. Carleton is the higher-leverage logo (Printice Gary's network, LIHTC validates the money math, multifamily is a bigger TAM) but the stakes are higher and the cents-precision spec needs to be airtight before they touch a pay app. Confirm which "Nexus" Walker meant before any outreach.
