## Critical Finding: Paradox of 100% Fix Rate with Declining Scores

Every cycle, the engine reports "100% fix rate" — all identified issues get a commit. But the overall module scores drop 3 to 7 points per cycle for 4 out of 11 modules. This means the "fixes" are net-negative: they introduce more problems than they solve.

### Root Causes:
1. **Code bloat**: Each fix adds lines. More code = more surface area for the auditor to penalize.
2. **Incomplete implementations**: Stub functions, placeholder UIs, and TODO comments score worse than no code.
3. **File thrashing**: Same files modified 10 to 18 times in 12 hours, each pass adding complexity without stabilizing.
4. **No revert mechanism**: When a fix makes a module's score worse, the engine never reverts it. It just adds more patches on top.
